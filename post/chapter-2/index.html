Premier League’s Top Performer: Data

July 15, 2020

Unlike basketball, football, and baseball that have several mechanisms at play to better predict a game, soccer has traditionally been known to be an ever changing sport that strays away from any sort of calculated strike to determine the match of a game. Finally, after years of spontaneity and tradition, data analytics has found its way into the hands of the English Premier League.

Through data-driven analysis, it has changed the way soccer is not just played, but managed. Premier League teams like Chelsea F.C. use data to scout young player’s potential through video analysis of youth matches. After going 30 years without winning the English Premier League, Liverpool were a team not to be feared on the field, but their recent rise back to world-class status by dominantly winning the Premier League in the 2019/2020 season is owed a major thanks to their data analytics team that have found rare trends better than any other team. They have coined the term of their playing, “pitch control” to determine places in the pitch that better their chances of scoring a goal within 15 seconds.

There is no doubt that soccer has shifted towards data-analytics. Data-driven sports like basketball and baseball have long been analyzed statistically. To now see a sport that has never bent to the rules of predictive statistics means that soccer is going to be more competitive and calculated than ever before.
